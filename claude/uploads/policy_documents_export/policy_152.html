SOCIAL MEDIA STRATEGY

Version: 2.0.1 | Last Updated: October 12, 2025

Dave Biggers for Mayor - Digital Campaign Blueprint

Candidate: Dave Biggers Website: rundaverun.org Purpose: Build digital presence, engage voters, amplify campaign messages online

---

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Social media is where most Louisville voters spend their time. This strategy turns digital platforms into campaign assets - building name recognition, sharing the vision, mobilizing volunteers, and driving votes.

Goals:

  • Build authentic online community
  • Reach 100,000 Louisville voters digitally
  • Convert followers to volunteers and voters
  • Amplify earned media and campaign events
  • Counter misinformation quickly

    • Platforms:

  • Facebook (primary - demographics match Louisville voters)
  • Instagram (secondary - visual storytelling)
  • Twitter/X (tertiary - media and influencer engagement)
  • TikTok (experimental - reach young voters)
  • YouTube (video hosting)

    RAPID RESPONSE PROTOCOL

    Monitoring:

    Tools:

  • Google Alerts for "Dave Biggers"
  • Social media monitoring (Hootsuite, Mention, etc.)
  • Media monitoring services
  • Opponent social media tracking
  • Comment section monitoring

    • Team:

  • Communications Director (final approval)
  • Social Media Manager (drafting)
  • Campaign Manager (strategic decisions)

    • Response Timeline:

    Tier 1: Immediate (Within 1 hour)

  • Major attacks from opponents
  • Misinformation going viral
  • Media misreporting
  • Crisis events

    • Tier 2: Same Day (Within 4 hours)

  • Opposition research
  • Negative news stories
  • Community concerns
  • Debate fact-checking

    • Tier 3: Next Day (Within 24 hours)

  • Minor attacks
  • Routine corrections
  • General pushback
  • Strategic positioning

    • Response Framework:

    1. Assess - Is this worth responding to? 2. Verify - Do we have the facts? 3. Strategize - What's the best response approach? 4. Draft - Write the response 5. Approve - Communications Director signs off 6. Post - Publish on appropriate platforms 7. Monitor - Track response and engagement

    Sample Rapid Responses:

    To Misinformation: "This is inaccurate. The facts: [Clear, cited facts]. We've published our complete budget at rundaverun.org/budget - 831 lines of detail. Transparency beats rumors."

    To Attacks: "My opponent wants to distract from their lack of a plan. Here's my detailed plan: [Link]. Let's talk about what works for Louisville, not political attacks."

    To Media Errors: "Correction: [Accurate information]. We're happy to provide additional details to any journalist covering this race. Facts matter."

    CRISIS MANAGEMENT ON SOCIAL MEDIA

    Potential Crises:

  • Dave makes a mistake in public
  • Staff member says something problematic
  • Supporter posts something offensive
  • Coordinated attack campaign
  • Misinformation goes viral
  • Opponent dumps opposition research

    • Response Protocol:

    Step 1: Pause (15-30 minutes)

  • Don't respond immediately
  • Gather facts
  • Assess severity
  • Convene crisis team

    • Step 2: Plan (30-60 minutes)

  • Determine response strategy
  • Draft statement
  • Identify platforms for response
  • Prepare for follow-up questions

    • Step 3: Respond

  • Post official statement
  • Brief Dave on talking points
  • Monitor response
  • Engage as appropriate

    • Step 4: Monitor & Adjust

  • Track sentiment
  • Address follow-up questions
  • Adjust strategy as needed
  • Document lessons learned

    • Crisis Communication Principles:

    ✅ Be transparent ✅ Take responsibility if appropriate ✅ Provide facts quickly ✅ Show empathy ✅ Stay on brand ✅ Don't fan the flames ✅ Know when silence is best
