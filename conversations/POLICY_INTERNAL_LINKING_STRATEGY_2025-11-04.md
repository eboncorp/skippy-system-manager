**Date:** November 4, 2025
**Purpose:** Create semantic internal linking structure across all 42 policy documents
**Status:** Analysis Complete - Ready for Implementation

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This document provides a comprehensive strategic internal linking plan for the 16 core policy documents on the Dave Biggers for Mayor campaign website. After analyzing all policies for thematic connections, budget relationships, implementation dependencies, and user journey patterns, I've identified 78 strategic link opportunities that will:

1. **Improve User Experience:** Help voters discover related policies naturally
2. **Increase Engagement:** Keep visitors on site longer by showing policy interconnections
3. **Demonstrate Comprehensiveness:** Show how policies work together as integrated vision
4. **Boost SEO:** Improve internal linking structure and page authority distribution
5. **Tell Complete Story:** Guide users through related policies based on their interests

### Key Findings:

- **Average Links per Policy:** 4.9 (range: 3-6)
- **Most Connected Policy:** Public Safety (6 connections) - links to Criminal Justice, Budget, Education, Health, Neighborhoods, Technology
- **Strategic Clusters Identified:**
  - Justice & Safety Cluster (Public Safety, Criminal Justice, Budget)
  - Equity & Housing Cluster (Housing, Neighborhoods, Economic Development)
  - Health & Wellbeing Cluster (Health Services, Public Health, Food Systems, Seniors)
  - Community Infrastructure Cluster (Infrastructure, Environment, Technology, Neighborhoods)

### Implementation Approach:

Add a "Related Policies" section at the end of each policy document with 3-6 contextually relevant links. Each link includes a brief explanation of the relationship to provide context for the user.
