**Created:** 2025-11-05
**Owner:** Campaign Leadership
**Status:** Active
**Priority:** HIGH

## Purpose

Standardize internal and external communication to ensure clear, consistent, and timely messaging across all channels while maintaining message quality and brand consistency.

**Focus:** Communication channels, approval workflows, crisis communication
**Integrates With:** incident_response_protocol, content_publishing_protocol, alert_management_protocol

---

## Scope

This protocol covers:
- Internal team communication
- External stakeholder communication
- Press and media relations
- Social media communication
- Crisis communication
- Email campaigns
- Website updates and announcements

---

## Communication Channels

### Internal Communication

#### **Tier 1: Urgent/Critical (Immediate Response Required)**
**Channels:**
- Phone call
- SMS/Text message
- Emergency Slack/Teams channel (@channel mention)

**Use For:**
- P0/P1 incidents
- Security breaches
- Site down
- Emergency decisions needed

**Response Time:** 15 minutes

---

#### **Tier 2: Important (Same-Day Response)**
**Channels:**
- Slack/Teams direct message
- Slack/Teams project channel
- Email (marked urgent)

**Use For:**
- P2 incidents
- Time-sensitive decisions
- Coordination during active work
- Schedule changes

**Response Time:** 4 hours (business hours)

---

#### **Tier 3: Standard (Next Business Day)**
**Channels:**
- Email
- Slack/Teams channel posts
- Project management tools

**Use For:**
- Project updates
- Non-urgent questions
- Documentation sharing
- Weekly reports

**Response Time:** 24 hours

---

#### **Tier 4: Informational (No Response Required)**
**Channels:**
- Email newsletters/digests
- Slack/Teams announcements channel
- Wiki/documentation updates

**Use For:**
- FYI updates
- Meeting notes
- Policy updates
- System status reports

**Response Time:** N/A

---

### External Communication

#### **Website/Blog Posts**
**Approval Required:** Campaign Manager
**Review Process:**
1. Draft in WordPress
2. Internal review (48 hours)
3. Campaign Manager approval
4. Schedule publication
5. Social media announcement

**Timeline:** Minimum 72 hours from draft to publish

---

#### **Social Media**
**Platforms:** Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, etc.
**Approval Required:**
- Routine posts: Communications Director
- Sensitive topics: Campaign Manager
- Crisis: Campaign Manager + Legal (if needed)

**Response Protocol:**
- Monitor mentions: Every 4 hours
- Respond to questions: Within 24 hours
- Escalate issues: Immediately

---

#### **Press/Media Relations**
**Approval Required:** Campaign Manager (ALWAYS)
**Designated Spokesperson:** [Designated Person]

**Process:**
1. Media inquiry received
2. Log inquiry (who, outlet, topic, deadline)
3. Notify Campaign Manager immediately
4. Draft response (if statement needed)
5. Manager approval
6. Designated spokesperson delivers response
7. Log outcome

**NEVER:**
- Respond to press without approval
- Provide "off the record" comments
- Speculate or guess

---

#### **Email Campaigns**
**Approval Required:** Campaign Manager or Communications Director
**Tools:** Mailgun, WordPress email plugins

**Process:**
1. Draft email content
2. Review for accuracy and tone
3. Test send to review team
4. Approval from manager
5. Schedule send
6. Monitor deliverability
7. Track metrics

**Timing:**
- Fundraising emails: Min 48-hour review
- Updates/newsletters: Min 72-hour review
- Urgent announcements: Min 24-hour review

## Crisis Communication

### Definition of Crisis

**A crisis is any situation that:**
- Threatens the campaign's reputation
- Involves legal issues
- Affects supporter safety or data
- Receives significant negative media attention
- Involves candidate directly

---

### Crisis Communication Team

**Core Team:**
- Campaign Manager (Lead)
- Communications Director
- Legal Counsel (if applicable)
- Technical Lead (if tech-related)
- Designated Spokesperson

**Responsibilities:**
- Assess situation severity
- Draft messaging
- Approve all communications
- Coordinate response
- Monitor public reaction

---

### Crisis Response Levels

#### **Level 1: Minor Issue**
**Examples:** Small website bug, single negative comment, minor scheduling issue
**Response:**
- Standard team handles
- Manager notified FYI
- Resolved through normal channels
**Communication:** Internal only

---

#### **Level 2: Moderate Issue**
**Examples:** Service disruption, data quality issue, critical bug, multiple complaints
**Response:**
- Manager directly involved
- Crisis team on standby
- Prepared statement drafted
- Monitor escalation
**Communication:** Internal + prepared external if asked

---

#### **Level 3: Serious Crisis**
**Examples:** Security breach, data leak, major system failure, significant negative press
**Response:**
- Full crisis team activated
- All external communication paused
- Coordinated response prepared
- Legal review required
- Spokesperson designated
**Communication:** Coordinated internal + external statements

---

### Crisis Communication Steps

**1. Immediate Actions (0-15 minutes):**
- Activate crisis team
- Gather facts
- Contain issue if technical
- Halt routine communications
- Prepare holding statement

**2. Initial Response (15-60 minutes):**
- Draft initial statement
- Legal review (if needed)
- Approve statement
- Publish to key channels
- Monitor response

**3. Ongoing Management (1-24 hours):**
- Regular updates (every 2-4 hours)
- Monitor all channels
- Respond to inquiries
- Update stakeholders
- Coordinate with legal/PR firms if needed

**4. Resolution & Recovery (24+ hours):**
- Final resolution statement
- Thank supporters
- Document lessons learned
- Update protocols
- Return to normal operations

---

### Crisis Communication Templates

#### **Holding Statement Template**
```
"We are aware of [issue] and are actively investigating. The [safety/security/integrity] of our [supporters/data/systems] is our top priority. We will provide updates as we have more information. For urgent questions, contact [email/phone]."
```

#### **Initial Response Template**
```
"Earlier today, [brief description of what happened]. We immediately [action taken]. Currently, [current status]. We are [ongoing actions]. We expect [timeline/resolution]. We apologize for [impact]. For questions, contact [email/phone]."
```

#### **Resolution Statement Template**
```
"Update on [issue]: As of [time], [issue] has been resolved. [What we did to fix it]. [What we're doing to prevent recurrence]. We sincerely apologize for [impact]. Thank you for your patience. Questions: [email/phone]."
```

## Message Guidelines

### Tone & Voice

**Campaign Voice:**
- Authentic and genuine
- Optimistic but realistic
- Inclusive and welcoming
- Professional but personable
- Action-oriented

**What to Avoid:**
- Jargon or insider language
- Overly formal/stiff tone
- Negativity or attacks (unless strategic)
- Ambiguity or vagueness
- Promises we can't keep

---

### Content Guidelines

**Every Message Should:**
- Have clear purpose
- Be concise and scannable
- Include call-to-action (if appropriate)
- Be fact-checked
- Match brand voice
- Be accessible (plain language)

**Every Message Must:**
- Be truthful and accurate
- Respect privacy
- Comply with legal requirements
- Be approved per workflow
- Be documented

---

## Social Media Guidelines

### Posting Schedule

**Optimal Times:**
- Morning: 7-9 AM (catching commute)
- Lunch: 12-1 PM (lunch break browsing)
- Evening: 6-8 PM (after work)

**Frequency:**
- Twitter: 3-5 times per day
- Facebook: 1-2 times per day
- Instagram: 1 time per day

---

### Engagement Guidelines

**DO:**
- Respond to genuine questions
- Thank supporters
- Share user-generated content (with permission)
- Use hashtags strategically
- Monitor mentions and tags
- Escalate issues promptly

**DON'T:**
- Engage with trolls
- Respond emotionally
- Make promises without approval
- Share unverified information
- Delete negative but legitimate feedback
- Ignore direct questions

---

### Social Media Crisis Protocol

**If negative trend emerges:**
1. Screenshot everything
2. Notify Communications Director immediately
3. Pause scheduled posts
4. DO NOT respond until approved
5. Monitor severity
6. Follow crisis communication protocol

## Email Campaign Guidelines

### Email Types

**1. Fundraising Emails**
- Subject: Urgent, personal, compelling
- Content: Short, emotional appeal
- CTA: Clear donation link
- Timing: Strategic (end of month, before deadline)

**2. Update Emails**
- Subject: Informative, newsworthy
- Content: Campaign progress, achievements
- CTA: Share, volunteer, learn more
- Timing: Weekly or bi-weekly

**3. Action Emails**
- Subject: Clear action requested
- Content: Why action matters, how to do it
- CTA: Specific action (sign petition, call, attend)
- Timing: Time-sensitive

---

### Email Best Practices

**Subject Lines:**
- 6-10 words optimal
- Personal, urgent, or intriguing
- Avoid spam trigger words
- Test A/B variations

**Content:**
- Mobile-friendly (60% read on mobile)
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences)
- Compelling opening
- Clear CTA above fold
- Personal sign-off

**Technical:**
- Test all links before sending
- Check rendering on mobile/desktop
- Verify unsubscribe link works
- Monitor deliverability rates
- Track open/click rates

## Incident Communication Integration

### When incident_response_protocol triggers:

**P0 (Critical) Incident:**
- Activate crisis communication team
- Prepare public statement (if public-facing)
- Update stakeholders every 30-60 minutes
- Follow crisis communication steps

**P1 (High) Incident:**
- Notify Communications Director
- Prepare statement draft (may not publish)
- Update stakeholders every 2-4 hours
- Monitor for escalation

**P2 (Medium) Incident:**
- Internal communication only (usually)
- Document for retrospective
- Publish update if user-facing

**P3 (Low) Incident:**
- Internal communication only
- Document in weekly report

## Metrics & KPIs

### Track These Metrics:

**Response Times:**
- Average response time to inquiries (target: <24 hours)
- Crisis activation time (target: <15 minutes)
- Statement approval time (target: <4 hours)

**Engagement:**
- Social media engagement rate (target: >2%)
- Email open rate (target: >20%)
- Email click-through rate (target: >3%)
- Message reach/impressions

**Quality:**
- Messages requiring correction (target: 0)
- Approval rejections (target: <5%)
- Supporter complaints about communication (target: <1%)
